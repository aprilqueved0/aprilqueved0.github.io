To my esteemed panel of Merrill faculty and alumni,

My family comes from a country in which journalists are killed in direct reprisal for their work every year. My grandfather, a Mexico City bus-driver-turned-Chicagoland-factory-worker, held journalists in high regard for that exact reason. “To be a journalist,” he would tell me, “you have to be fast, you have to be smart and you have to be brave.”

For eight years, I worked in logistics and supply chain management. Through my work, I developed a deep understanding of transportation systems and supply chain dynamics. Most importantly, I learned the incredible power and value of data and how to use it to effectively craft and deliver narratives to an audience.

As I prepare to graduate, I am excited to present my portfolio to you. It reflects not only the skills I have developed and refined at Merrill but also my commitment to investigative journalism and to serving underrepresented communities through accessible storytelling. I deeply appreciate the opportunity to share my work and to reflect on how Merrill has provided a strong foundation and will continue to shape my career in journalism.

I welcome any feedback or questions.

Thank you,
April Quevedo
